About Me

I'm Majedul Shihab, a BSc student in Genetic Engineering & Biotechnology at East West University, Dhaka — currently maintaining a CGPA of 3.64/4.00 and recognized on the Dean's List.

My research interests lie at the intersection of precision genome engineering and translational medicine, with a focus on gene-editing technologies that can be deployed as targeted therapeutic tools. I aim to bridge the gap between foundational molecular science and real-world clinical application.

I'm currently conducting undergraduate research on identifying plastic-degrading enzymes from environmental bacterial samples using metagenomic analysis — a project with tangible environmental relevance for Bangladesh and beyond.

Beyond the bench, I'm an active leader in the biotech student community — serving as Treasurer of the NYBB student chapter and Sub-Executive Member of the EWU Biotech Club.

My Work

Research Experience

Ongoing · Metagenomics

Identifying Plastic-Degrading Enzymes via Metagenomic Analysis

Jan 2025 – Present · East West University · Supervisor: Dr. MST Murshida Mahbub, Dept. of GEB

This undergraduate research project aims to identify plastic-degrading enzymes from bacterial samples obtained from a waste-dumping site in Bangladesh. Using metagenomic analysis, the study seeks to characterize the enzymatic machinery that enables microbial communities to break down plastics — offering potential biotechnological solutions for environmental remediation and sustainable waste management.
